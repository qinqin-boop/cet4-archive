Passage 1: 关于阅读习惯

A growing body of research suggests that the medium we read in shapes how deeply we understand what we read. When students read long passages on paper, they tend to recall main arguments and supporting details with greater accuracy than when they read the same passages on a screen. Researchers attribute this to several factors. First, paper offers spatial cues — a reader instinctively remembers that a key idea appeared "near the top of the left page" — that screens lack. Second, scrolling encourages skimming, while turning pages encourages reflection.

However, screen reading is not without its merits. Searchability, hyperlinks, and the ability to highlight and annotate digitally make it superior for research tasks. The challenge for educators is to teach students when to read on paper and when to read on screen. Deep comprehension of a literary novel may demand paper; quickly locating a fact in a textbook may favor a tablet.

Perhaps the most surprising finding is that students themselves often overestimate their understanding when reading on screens. They believe they have grasped the material, but tests reveal otherwise. This metacognitive illusion suggests that simply telling students to "read more carefully" online may not help; what is needed is a slower, more deliberate reading practice regardless of medium.
题 1

What does research suggest about reading on paper vs. screen?

A. Paper reading is always better.
B. Screen reading recalls more details.
C. Paper reading tends to produce better recall of arguments and details.
D. There is no difference between the two.

📖 答案 / 解析

答案: C

解析: 文章第一句直接给出结论。注意 A 选项 "always" 过于绝对,C 是 paragraph 1 的核心。

Passage 2: 关于职场远程办公

The rapid spread of remote work has changed how cities function. Office vacancy rates in major financial districts have reached levels not seen in decades, while suburban and small-town housing markets have boomed. Workers who once commuted ninety minutes each way are now buying larger homes in quieter areas, prioritizing space and natural surroundings over closeness to the workplace.

This shift produces mixed consequences. Coffee shops, dry cleaners, and lunch counters that depended on weekday office foot traffic are struggling. Many city governments have begun encouraging the conversion of empty office buildings into residential apartments, both to revive downtowns and to ease housing shortages. The economics are tricky: floor plans designed for cubicles do not easily accommodate kitchens and bathrooms.

Meanwhile, hybrid work — coming into the office two or three days a week — is settling in as a new norm. Researchers find that fully remote workers report higher loneliness, while fully in-office workers report less flexibility and family time. A balanced rhythm appears to offer the best of both worlds, though the optimal mix varies by industry and personality.

Passage 3: 关于个人理财

For most young adults, the most powerful tool for building long-term wealth is not stock picking, real estate speculation, or running a side business. It is the boringly simple habit of saving a consistent fraction of every paycheck and investing it in low-cost index funds. The math behind this is straightforward: even modest sums, invested early, grow dramatically over decades because of compound interest.

Consider two recent college graduates. Anna starts setting aside two hundred dollars a month at age twenty-two and continues for the next forty-three years. Ben, slightly more impatient, waits until thirty-five to begin, then contributes four hundred dollars a month for the next thirty years. Despite Ben contributing more total money, Anna ends up with substantially more savings because her contributions had more time to grow.

The trickiest part of investing is psychological, not technical. Markets fluctuate, and during downturns the temptation to sell and "wait until things calm down" is enormous. Yet historical evidence is clear: those who stay invested through market storms almost always come out ahead of those who try to time the market. Knowing this in theory and acting on it during a real crash are very different things.
