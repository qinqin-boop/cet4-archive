Section A · Listening

听力 题 1
W: I'm submitting my thesis on Friday. Could you read the introduction tonight? M: Sorry, I have an exam tomorrow morning and need to focus. How about Thursday afternoon? W: That cuts it close, but it should work. M: Email it over and I'll give you detailed notes.

Q: When will the man read the woman's introduction?

正确答案: C

解析: Thursday afternoon。

听力 题 2
M: Did you finish the reading list for tomorrow's seminar? W: Honestly, I only got through half. The third article was so technical that I had to look up every other word. M: I had the same problem. Maybe we should form a study group to share notes. W: Great idea. Let's invite Lisa and Tom too.

Q: What does the woman agree to do?

正确答案: B

解析: Great idea + Let's invite Lisa and Tom too。

听力 题 3
W: I read that universities are now offering short, intensive courses during winter breaks. M: Yes, mini-terms. They typically last two to four weeks and focus on one topic. W: Sounds productive but exhausting. M: Some students love it for the focus, others find the pace too fast.

Q: How long does a typical mini-term last?

正确答案: B

解析: two to four weeks 直接命中。

听力 题 4
W: My roommate plays music loudly until midnight. I haven't slept well in a week. M: Have you told her directly that it bothers you? W: I dropped a few hints, but she didn't get them. M: Hints don't work. Talk to her plainly when you're both calm — most roommate problems come from indirect communication.

Q: What does the man recommend?

正确答案: B

解析: Talk to her plainly when you're both calm。

听力 题 5
M: I haven't seen you in the cafeteria all week. W: I've been packing lunch from home to save money. M: How much are you saving? W: About fifty yuan a week. It adds up to over two thousand a year. M: That's a smart move. I might try it.

Q: Why is the woman packing lunch?

正确答案: B

解析: save money + fifty yuan a week 直接命中。

Section B · Reading

Passage: Compound Interest for Young Investors

For most young adults, the most powerful tool for building long-term wealth is the boringly simple habit of saving a consistent fraction of every paycheck and investing in low-cost index funds. Modest sums invested early grow dramatically over decades. Consider Anna who starts saving 200 dollars a month at age 22 for 43 years; Ben waits until 35 then contributes 400 a month for 30 years. Despite Ben contributing more, Anna ends up with substantially more because her money had more years to compound. The trickiest part of investing is psychological — staying invested during market downturns matters more than picking the right stocks.
阅读 题 1

Most powerful wealth tool for young adults?

正确答案: C

解析: 原文首句明确。

阅读 题 2

Why does Anna end up with more savings?

正确答案: B

解析: money had more years to compound。

阅读 题 3

At what age did Anna start saving?

正确答案: B

解析: 原文 age 22。

阅读 题 4

What is the trickiest part of investing?

正确答案: B

解析: trickiest part of investing is psychological。

Passage: Smart Cities and Privacy

Smart cities promise smoother traffic, cleaner air, and faster emergency response by collecting data from sensors, cameras, and citizens' phones. The trade-off is privacy. When a city knows where every bus, car, and pedestrian is at all times, it also knows where every person travels. Citizens often consent to data collection without understanding what is gathered or how long it is stored. Responsible smart-city design publishes clear data policies, sets short retention windows, and allows residents to opt out of non-essential tracking. Privacy and efficiency are not opposites, but they require careful design choices.
阅读 题 1

What do smart cities promise?

正确答案: B

解析: 原文首句。

阅读 题 2

What is the trade-off?

正确答案: B

解析: The trade-off is privacy。

阅读 题 3

What problem with citizen consent does the passage raise?

正确答案: B

解析: consent without understanding。

阅读 题 4

What does responsible smart-city design include?

正确答案: B

解析: publishes clear data policies, sets short retention windows。

请将下列中文译成英文:

Section D · Writing

写作 题 1

Write an essay (120-180 words) on "Should Universities Limit AI Tools in Writing Assignments?".

参考范文: AI writing tools have forced universities to ask: how much help is too much? Banning AI altogether seems unrealistic; the technology is already part of professional life. Yet unrestricted use risks producing graduates who cannot write a single original sentence under pressure. A reasonable middle path distinguishes between core writing courses where AI should be off-limits, and editing or research stages where AI can speed routine work.

解析: 中间立场。

写作 题 2

Write an essay (120-180 words) on "The Role of Sports in University Life".

参考范文: Regular sports matter as much as lectures. Physical activity improves not only fitness but also concentration and mood, both directly supporting academic performance. Joining a basketball team or running club expands one's social circle. The simplest entry is the lowest barrier: a thirty-minute jog three times a week, a campus yoga class, or weekend hiking. Sports are not a distraction from study but an investment.

解析: investment that pays off。
