Section A · Listening

听力 题 1
M: I want to start volunteering on weekends. Any suggestions? W: There's a community garden project that teaches low-income families to grow vegetables. M: That sounds rewarding. How many hours per week? W: Just three or four. They want consistent help rather than long shifts.

Q: What volunteer commitment does the program prefer?

正确答案: C

解析: consistent help + three or four hours per week。

听力 题 2
W: Did you go to the career fair yesterday? M: Yes, but it was so crowded that I only managed to talk to two companies. W: Which ones impressed you? M: A small startup actually. The founder explained their product better than any big-firm recruiter.

Q: Which company impressed the man most?

正确答案: A

解析: A small startup actually + founder explained better。

听力 题 3
M: I got two internship offers — one at a bank, one at a tech startup. Which would you pick? W: Depends on what you want. The bank pays better and looks great on a resume, but the startup gives you real responsibility from day one. M: I think I'd grow faster at the startup. W: Then trust that instinct.

Q: Which internship does the man lean toward?

正确答案: B

解析: I'd grow faster at the startup。

听力 题 4
W: I'm writing my graduate school personal statement and it feels stiff. M: Show, don't tell. Instead of writing 'I am passionate about research', tell the moment a research result actually surprised you. W: That makes sense. Specific stories beat general adjectives. M: Exactly. Admissions officers read thousands of statements that say 'I am passionate'.

Q: What writing advice does the man give?

正确答案: B

解析: Show, don't tell + Specific stories beat general adjectives。

听力 题 5
W: My roommate plays music loudly until midnight. I haven't slept well in a week. M: Have you told her directly that it bothers you? W: I dropped a few hints, but she didn't get them. M: Hints don't work. Talk to her plainly when you're both calm — most roommate problems come from indirect communication.

Q: What does the man recommend?

正确答案: B

解析: Talk to her plainly when you're both calm。

Section B · Reading

Passage: Lifelong Learning

The skills that earn a comfortable salary today may be obsolete in a decade. Automation and shifting job markets mean a diploma at 22 is no longer a lifetime ticket. Lifelong learning has moved from a nice-to-have to an essential survival skill. Fortunately, free online courses, podcasts, and communities of practice offer expert content at almost no cost. The challenge is no longer access but discipline. Setting aside five hours a week for deliberate study keeps one relevant, curious, and confident over decades of change.
阅读 题 1

Why is lifelong learning more important now?

正确答案: B

解析: may be obsolete in a decade。

阅读 题 2

Is access the biggest challenge today?

正确答案: B

解析: challenge is no longer access but discipline。

阅读 题 3

What weekly time commitment is suggested?

正确答案: B

解析: five hours a week。

阅读 题 4

'Obsolete' means closest to:

正确答案: B

解析: obsolete = no longer useful。

Passage: The Future of Books

Some predicted that e-books would kill paper books, but the data tells a more interesting story. After early excitement, e-book sales plateaued and paper sales have actually grown in many markets. Readers report that paper books invite slower, more reflective reading, while e-readers excel for travel and instant access. The two formats coexist for different needs. Independent bookstores have rebounded by offering events, curated recommendations, and a community space that online retailers cannot replicate. Paper books, far from dying, have found a renewed value as a quiet alternative to screens.
阅读 题 1

What did some people predict?

正确答案: B

解析: predicted that e-books would kill paper。

阅读 题 2

What does the data actually show?

正确答案: B

解析: After early excitement, e-book sales plateaued。

阅读 题 3

Why have independent bookstores rebounded?

正确答案: B

解析: events, curated recommendations, and a community space。

阅读 题 4

What value of paper books does the author highlight?

正确答案: B

解析: a quiet alternative to screens。

Section C · Translation

翻译 题 1

请将下列中文译成英文:

故宫位于北京市中心,曾是中国明清两代二十四位皇帝的居所。它始建于一四〇六年,占地超过七十二万平方米,共有近九千间房屋。

参考译文: The Forbidden City, located in the heart of Beijing, was once the residence of twenty-four emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Construction began in 1406. It covers more than 720,000 square meters with nearly 9,000 rooms.

解析: 故宫 The Forbidden City / 明清 Ming and Qing dynasties。

翻译 题 2

请将下列中文译成英文:

京剧是中国最具代表性的传统戏剧形式之一,已有两百多年历史。它融合了唱、念、做、打四种基本表现手法。

参考译文: Peking Opera is one of the most iconic traditional Chinese theatrical forms, with more than two hundred years of history. It combines four basic performance techniques: singing, speech, acting, and martial arts.

解析: 京剧 Peking Opera。

翻译 题 3

请将下列中文译成英文:

共享单车在中国城市迅速普及。用户通过手机扫码即可解锁车辆,骑行后任意停放。这种方式既方便了短距离出行,也减少了汽车带来的污染。

参考译文: Shared bikes have rapidly spread across Chinese cities. Users can unlock a bike by scanning a QR code on their phone and park it anywhere after riding. This model has made short-distance travel more convenient while reducing pollution from cars.

解析: 共享单车 shared bikes / 短距离出行 short-distance travel。

Section D · Writing

写作 题 1

Write an essay (120-180 words) on "Travel and Personal Growth".

参考范文: Travel does more than fill a photo album. Stepping outside one's daily routine forces a person to navigate new languages, foods, and customs. A first solo trip sharpens decision-making: one must read maps, manage money, and recover from inevitable mistakes alone. Even short trips broaden perspective. After eating with a local family abroad, one may question what 'ordinary food' means. Travel is not escape from real life but training for it.

解析: 成长意义。

写作 题 2

Write an essay (120-180 words) on "The Importance of Mental Health Awareness on Campus".

参考范文: Behind busy schedules, mental health problems are quietly common among students. Pressure from grades, employment, and social comparison can accumulate into anxiety long before anyone notices. Universities have a clear responsibility: visible counseling services, peer-support groups, and stress workshops should be routine, not crisis-only. Students themselves can build small habits - adequate sleep, exercise, honest conversations - that prevent problems from growing. Asking for help is a sign of strength.

解析: 心理健康。
