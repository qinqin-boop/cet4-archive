## 原创仿真模拟卷

### 写作 Part(站长原创题目)

::ex
**题目**:Write a short essay (120-180 words) on *"Balancing Study and Leisure in College Life"*. Include:
1. Why balance matters during four years of university.
2. Common signs that the balance is off (例如 burnout 或 procrastination).
3. Practical strategies to maintain it.

**答案**:范文骨架 — 三段式:(1) 大学四年时间窗口短,过度学习导致 burnout,过度娱乐导致 GPA 滑坡;(2) 失衡信号:连续熬夜赶 deadline / 一周不进图书馆 / 兴趣全部转为短视频;(3) 策略:固定一天作"非工作日"专门娱乐,固定一段"深度工作时间"关掉社交 app,加入一个既是社交又有学习元素的社团。

**解析**:写作主题"平衡/balance"类话题套路通用,先承认"both extremes are problematic",然后给具体可行的对策(不要泛泛而谈),最后回扣"long-term goal of self-development"。
::end

### 翻译 Part(原创)

::ex
**题目**:请将下列中文译成英文(~100 字)。

> 茶在中国有着四千多年的饮用历史。中国茶分为六大类:绿茶、红茶、乌龙茶、白茶、黄茶和黑茶。每一类茶的发酵程度不同,因此口感和颜色也各不相同。在中国,泡茶不仅是一种解渴方式,也是一种待客之道与生活仪式。无论是简单的盖碗,还是讲究的紫砂壶,每一道工序都体现了中国人对慢生活的追求。

**答案**:*Tea has been consumed in China for more than four thousand years. Chinese tea is classified into six categories: green, black, oolong, white, yellow, and dark tea. Each category undergoes a different degree of fermentation, resulting in distinct flavors and colors. In China, brewing tea is not merely a way to quench thirst but also a way of welcoming guests and a daily ritual. Whether using a simple covered bowl (gaiwan) or a refined Yixing purple clay pot, every step reflects the Chinese pursuit of a slow, contemplative way of life.*

**解析**:茶类术语:绿茶 green tea / 红茶 black tea(注意:中国"红茶"对应英文 black tea,因茶汤颜色)/ 乌龙茶 oolong / 白茶 white / 黄茶 yellow / 黑茶 dark tea。专有名词盖碗 gaiwan、紫砂壶 Yixing purple clay pot 用拼音 + 描述。
::end

### 阅读 Part(原创短文)

::ex
**Passage**(原创,~220 词,主题 AI 与就业):

> The rapid advance of artificial intelligence is transforming the job market faster than many earlier waves of automation. Unlike mechanical robots that replaced manual labor in factories, modern AI systems can now draft emails, summarize legal documents, and write computer code. This shift threatens not only routine clerical positions but also white-collar work that was once considered safe.
>
> However, the picture is not entirely bleak. AI tends to take over narrow, repetitive sub-tasks rather than entire jobs. A copywriter, for example, may still be needed to provide creative direction even as AI generates first drafts. Lawyers continue to interpret cases and persuade juries, though they spend less time on document review. The net effect is that many jobs are being redesigned rather than eliminated.
>
> Workers most likely to thrive in this new environment are those who combine technical literacy with soft skills — communication, empathy, and creative problem-solving. Universities have begun to adjust by introducing data-literacy requirements for all majors, including humanities and arts. Lifelong learning, once considered optional, is now becoming essential.

**Question 1**: How is the current AI wave different from earlier automation?

A. It replaces manual labor only.
B. It can handle creative writing and code drafting, threatening white-collar work too.
C. It is slower than mechanical automation.
D. It only affects students.

**答案**:B

**解析**:第一段对比 modern AI 与 mechanical robots 的关键区别就是 draft emails / write code,这是 white-collar 工作。
::end

::ex
**Question 2**: What is the author's overall attitude toward AI's impact on jobs?

A. Entirely pessimistic.
B. Entirely optimistic.
C. Nuanced — some risks, but also opportunities for redesigned work.
D. Indifferent.

**答案**:C

**解析**:文章承认 "threats" 但也说 "not entirely bleak" 与 "redesigned rather than eliminated"。这是态度题,选最折中、最贴合全文的。
::end
