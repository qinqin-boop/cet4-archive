## 原创仿真模拟卷

### 写作 Part(站长原创题目)

::ex
**题目**:Write a short essay (120-180 words) on *"Should Universities Limit AI Tools in Writing Assignments?"*. Include:
1. The strongest argument for limiting AI use in coursework.
2. The strongest argument against such limits.
3. Your own balanced position.

**答案**:范文骨架 — (1) 反对侧:AI 可能稀释学生的独立思考训练,导致 "graduates who can't think without prompts";(2) 支持侧:AI 是新时代的工具,禁止反而让学生缺乏 employability;(3) 中间立场:核心写作课限制 AI(锻炼基本功),非核心 / 编辑润色阶段允许 AI,关键是教学生**正确使用**而非禁绝。

**解析**:CET-4 议论文若是争议话题,选"中庸立场"+ 具体边界比单纯赞同/反对得分更高,因为体现 critical thinking。
::end

### 翻译 Part(原创)

::ex
**题目**:请将下列中文译成英文(~100 字)。

> 京剧是中国最具代表性的传统戏剧形式之一,已有两百多年历史。它融合了唱、念、做、打四种基本表现手法,角色分为生、旦、净、丑四大行当。京剧演员的脸谱色彩具有象征意义:红色代表忠诚,黑色代表正直,白色则常用于反派人物。二〇一〇年,京剧被联合国教科文组织列入人类非物质文化遗产代表作名录。

**答案**:*Peking Opera is one of the most iconic traditional Chinese theatrical forms, with a history of more than two hundred years. It combines four basic performance techniques: singing, speech, acting, and martial arts. The roles are divided into four main categories — sheng (male), dan (female), jing (painted-face), and chou (clown). The colors of the facial makeup carry symbolic meanings: red represents loyalty, black represents integrity, and white is often used for villainous characters. In 2010, Peking Opera was inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.*

**解析**:行当术语用拼音 + 括号英文解释,这是 CET-4 文化词的标准处理。
::end

### 阅读 Part(原创短文)

::ex
**Passage**(原创,~190 词,主题生成式 AI):

> Generative AI has moved from research labs into ordinary classrooms within just a few years. Many universities now grapple with a difficult question: when does student use of AI cross the line from helpful tool to academic dishonesty?
>
> One emerging approach distinguishes between "ideation" and "execution". Using AI to brainstorm essay topics or to clarify confusing concepts is generally accepted, since these activities resemble talking with a friend or consulting a tutor. Submitting AI-generated text as one's own original work, however, is widely treated as plagiarism. Some schools require students to disclose any AI assistance and attach the relevant prompts to the final submission.
>
> Critics argue that such policies are difficult to enforce: detection software is unreliable, and a student who carefully edits AI output may be indistinguishable from one who wrote everything by hand. Proponents counter that the goal is not perfect detection but a shared norm — making students consciously choose how they want to engage with AI as part of their education.

**Question 1**: What is the main distinction the article draws regarding AI use?

A. Free vs. paid tools
B. Ideation vs. execution
C. Undergraduate vs. graduate
D. Lab vs. classroom

**答案**:B

**解析**:第二段开头 "distinguishes between 'ideation' and 'execution'" 是 topic sentence,直接给出答案。
::end

## 备考建议(决赛冲刺)

考前 30 天冲刺计划:
